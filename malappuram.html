MALAPPURAM - "City Of Hills"


Malappuram is one of the 14 districts in the Indian state of Kerala, with a coastline of 70 km. It is the most populous district of Kerala, which is home to around 13% of the total population of the state. The district was formed on 16 June 1969.
Adyanpara Falls is a waterfall in the Kurumbalangode village of Nilambur subdistrict. Arimbra Hills, also known as 'Mini-Ooty', since it resembles Ooty. It is at a height of 1050 feet above sea level. Arya Vaidya Sala is known for its heritage and expertise in the Indian traditional medicine system of Ayurveda. Bharathappuzha is the longest river of Kerala. Also known in the names River Ponnani, Nila and Perar.
