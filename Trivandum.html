TRIVANDRUM - "The Town Of Lord ANANTHA"


Thiruvananthapuram,commonly known by its former name Trivandrum,is the capital of the Indian state of Kerala.The encompassing urban agglomeration population is around 1.68 million.Referred to by Mahatma Gandhi as the "Evergreen city of India", the city is characterised by its undulating terrain of low coastal hills.
Thiruvananthapuram is a major tourist hub in India. Kovalam and Varkala are popular beach towns located near the city. Other important beaches include Poovar, Shankumugham Beach, Azhimala Beach, Vizhinjam Beach and Veli Beach.The Padmanabhaswamy Temple located at the heart of the city is known as the richest place of worship in the world.Other places of interest include Agasthyamala rain forests, Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, Kallar, Braemore, Ponmudi hills, Poovar, Anchuthengu backwaters, Varkala Cliffs and Kappil-Edava lakes.
